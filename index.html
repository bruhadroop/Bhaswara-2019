Civil Paper Presentation

Rules and Regulations

  • Registration Fee: Rs. 100/- per head
  • The number of students in each contribution and presentation should not exceed 2
  • The paper must be in IEEE format (not exceeding 6 pages) including synopsis/abstract in ONE paragraph.
  • The paper must be sent to bhaswara@gcet.edu.in. Deadline for submission Mar 2- 2019.
  • The selection will be intimated to the presenters on or before through mail / phone.
  • The selected participants should submit ONE hard copy of paper at the time of presentation. 10 Minutes Presentation for each paper (including Q&A and clarifications by the Judges).
  • The oral presentation should be in the form of Microsoft PowerPoint slides not exceeding TEN slides (excluding a Title slide and a "Thanks for patient listening" slide, focusing mainly on the contribution made by the authors.
  • The papers will be judged on the basis of their depth of the knowledge in the field and presentation skills.
  • The decision of the judges will be final and binding.

Topics for Presentation

  1. Remote Sensing and GIS
  2. Surveying
  3. Srtuctural Analysis and Designing
  4. Building Materials
  5. Water Resources
  6. Geo Technical Engineering
  7. General Topics related to Civil Engineering
  8. Environmental Engineering
  9. Other new topics will also be considered

Judging Criteria

Max. Marks: 50 for Presentation
  1. Depth of Content (10 Marks)
    • Content deals with an important issue in the field of study, includes goals/motivating questions that provide the audience with a sense of the project’s main idea.
    • Information is accurate and includes a complete explanation of key concepts and theories drawing upon relevant literature, as well as a clear description of the results and the importance of the results.
    • Applications of theory are included to illuminate issues.
  2. Organization (10 Marks)
    • Presentation is clear, logical and organized. Listener can follow line of reasoning.
  3. Presentation Style (10 Marks)
    • Level of presentation is appropriate for the audience.
    • Presentation is a planned conversation, paced for audience understanding.
    • Speaker is clearly comfortable in front of the group and can be heard by all.
  4. Verbal Interaction (10 Marks)
    • Consistently clarifies, restates, and responds to questions.
    • Summarizes when needed
  5. Communication Aids (10 Marks)
    • Communication aids are prepared in a professional manner and enhance the presentation.
    • Information is organized to maximize audience understanding.
    • Details are minimized so that main points stand out.

Civil Poster Presentation

Information

  • Registration Fee: Rs. 100/- per head
  • All posters must be original work, prepared by a max. of 2 applicant(s) and not previously presented at any other competition.
  • Student registrant(s) may only submit or be a participant on one poster.
  • Specifications: Size: A3 chart.
  • Presentation: Landscape; Bordered; Colour and/or Black and White.
  • Preferred to Have:
    1. Society
    2. Tecnhical
    3. Cultural
    4. Message Oriented Posters
    5. Participants should not use abusive posters
  • Should Not Use: Paragraph type text

Rules

    Subject matter for the poster will be chosen by the presenter and should be pertinent to a study
  1. There is no limitation on how many colours can be used on the poster.
  2. Colour of the actual poster board can be any colour as long as it fits the dimensions mentioned above.
  3. All mediums can be used (markers, crayons, pastels, paint, print etc.).
  4. Original photography, magazine clippings, stencils, buttons may be utilized on the poster.
  5. There are no specifications for text/font size.
  6. Text should be of sufficient size for easy reading at a distance.

Judging Criteria

  1. Coverage of Topic (10 Marks): Details on the poster captures the important information about the topic and increases the audiences’ understanding.
  2. Layout & Design (10 Marks):  All information on the poster is in focus and can be easily viewed and identified from a distance.
  3. Mechanics (10 Marks):  No grammatical, spelling or punctuation errors.
  4. Presentation (10 Marks): The presentation should be appropriate length. It should not seem hurried or too slow. The presenter clearly and distinctly established connection with the audience
