Rules and Regulations

  • Registration Fee: Rs. 100/- per head
  • The number of students in each contribution and presentation should not exceed 2
  • The paper must be in IEEE format (not exceeding 6 pages) including synopsis/abstract in ONE paragraph.
  • The paper must be sent to bhaswara@gcet.edu.in. Deadline for submission Mar 2- 2019.
  • The selection will be intimated to the presenters on or before through mail / phone.
  • The selected participants should submit ONE hard copy of paper at the time of presentation. 10 Minutes Presentation for each paper (including Q&A and clarifications by the Judges).
  • The oral presentation should be in the form of Microsoft PowerPoint slides not exceeding TEN slides (excluding a Title slide and a "Thanks for patient listening" slide, focusing mainly on the contribution made by the authors.
  • The papers will be judged on the basis of their depth of the knowledge in the field and presentation skills.
  • The decision of the judges will be final and binding.

Topics for Presentation

  1. Remote Sensing and GIS
  2. Surveying
  3. Srtuctural Analysis and Designing
  4. Building Materials
  5. Water Resources
  6. Geo Technical Engineering
  7. General Topics related to Civil Engineering
  8. Environmental Engineering
  9. Other new topics will also be considered

Judging Criteria

Max. Marks: 50 for Presentation
  1. Depth of Content (10 Marks)
    • Content deals with an important issue in the field of study, includes goals/motivating questions that provide the audience with a sense of the project’s main idea.
    • Information is accurate and includes a complete explanation of key concepts and theories drawing upon relevant literature, as well as a clear description of the results and the importance of the results.
    • Applications of theory are included to illuminate issues.
  2. Organization (10 Marks)
    • Presentation is clear, logical and organized. Listener can follow line of reasoning.
  3. Presentation Style (10 Marks)
    • Level of presentation is appropriate for the audience.
    • Presentation is a planned conversation, paced for audience understanding.
    • Speaker is clearly comfortable in front of the group and can be heard by all.
  4. Verbal Interaction (10 Marks)
    • Consistently clarifies, restates, and responds to questions.
    • Summarizes when needed
  5. Communication Aids (10 Marks)
    • Communication aids are prepared in a professional manner and enhance the presentation.
    • Information is organized to maximize audience understanding.
    • Details are minimized so that main points stand out.

Circuit Chess

Rules and Regulations

    Registration Fee: Rs. 50/- per head.
  1. Round 1: Guess Me
    • A group of 2 or 3 students are made as a team.
    • The components or devices will be displayed on the projector screen.
    • Participants need to guess the name of component/device and arrange the name using alphabet blocks given to them in specified time limit.
    • Points will be awarded based on the time taken by the team to complete the task.
    • Teams with more points are qualified to the next round.
  2. Round 2: Circuit Hunt
    • Each team need to select a envelope which consists of a name of the circuit and their first hint.
    • Using the hints, they need to search for all the components list.
    • After finding all the chits i.e name of the components, they need to submit all the chits to the coordinator.
    • All the components will be provided to the team along with the circuit diagram.
    • They need to assemble the circuit
    • Points will be allotted to each team based on the time taken by them to complete the task.
  3. Round 3: Tricky Circuit
    • Teams which are qualified in Round 2 can only participate.
    • A circuit is displayed on the projector screen
    • All the participants need to assemble the circuit on breadboard in specified time limit
    • Participants who complete the task in less time are declared as winners.
    • Judge decision will be is the FINAL.

Technical Tambola

Description

This game is similar to the tambola game but instead of reading out the numbers we have few technical questions with respect to Electronics, Computers and Maths which can be solved in 30seconds – 1 minute The team which solves the questions and strikes off all the numbers what they get in the tambola paper then they are considered to be the winners.

  • A team can consist of one or two members.
  • The Number of questions to be solved can range from 15-20.
  • The team which solves all the questions(puzzles) and strikes off the answers in their paper before everyone, they are considered to be the winners.
  • The team with second highest number of strike-offs are considered to be the runners.
  • If the number of students participating are 20-30 we consider them as one batch, if there are more than 20-30 we will have two batches and two different set of 15 questions.
  • If there is a tie between two more teams we will be having extra questions for them to decide the winners. So number of rounds are dependent on this factor.
  • Participation fee to be charged can be 50 per Head.
  • Judge decision will be the FINAL.
